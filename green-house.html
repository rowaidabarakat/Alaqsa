Greenhouse Farming: A Revolution in Modern Agriculture
Introduction to Greenhouse Farming
Greenhouse farming is an advanced agricultural technique that provides a controlled environment for plant growth, protecting crops from harsh weather conditions and pests. A greenhouse is a structure made of glass or plastic that traps solar radiation, creating a warm and stable climate ideal for plant development. This method allows for year-round cultivation, higher yields, and better-quality crops, making it a crucial innovation in modern agriculture.

Uses of Greenhouses in Agriculture
1- Year-Round Cultivation One of the primary benefits of greenhouse farming is the ability to grow crops in all seasons, regardless of external weather conditions. By regulating temperature, humidity, and light, farmers can cultivate fruits, vegetables, and flowers even in regions with extreme climates.
2- Growing High-Value Crops Greenhouses are commonly used to grow high-value crops that require specific conditions, such as:

4- Water Efficiency & Drip Irrigation Greenhouse farming often incorporates drip irrigation systems, which supply water directly to plant roots, reducing water wastage and enhancing nutrient absorption. This method is particularly beneficial in regions facing water scarcity.
